Puspa Poudel

Where Nepali heartbeats become words
and silence turns into stories.

A voice rooted in the mountains and valleys of Nepal, Puspa Poudel weaves novels, short literature, and poetry that explore human longing, identity, and the gentle passage of time. Her work has moved readers across generations with its honest beauty.

"I write not to escape the world, but to understand it — one line at a time."
— Puspa Poudel

Puspa Poudel is a celebrated Nepali author known for her deeply human prose and lyrical poetry. Born and raised in Nepal, she draws from the rich oral traditions, landscapes, and lived experiences of her homeland to create literature that resonates far beyond borders.

Her novels explore the intricate emotional lives of ordinary people — mothers, daughters, lovers, wanderers — with a tenderness that is distinctly her own. Her poetry collections have been praised for their spare, meditative quality and the way they hold grief and joy in the same breath.

Over the years, Puspa has contributed to literary journals, participated in national literature festivals, and mentored emerging Nepali writers. She believes that storytelling is a sacred act — a bridge between the self and the world.
